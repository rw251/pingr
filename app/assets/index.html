About PINGR

PINGR analyses coded data from primary care electronic health records to suggest quality improvements GP practices could make in the way they care for long-term conditions. Health record data are compared against quality standards from the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) or the Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) according to 4 aspects of care:

  • Diagnosis: How well a condition is diagnosed.
  • Monitoring: How well a condition is monitored.
  • Treatment: How well a condition is treated.
  • Exclusions: Whether patients are appropriately excluded from quality standards.

Based on this information, PINGR then suggests quality improvement actions a GP practice could undertake as a practice and for individual patients.

How to use PINGR

  • Select the clinical area you wish to look at from the left hand menu. When you’ve done this, the analysis of the 4 aspects of care for that condition in your practice will be displayed.
  • How PINGR thinks care could be improved can be found by clicking on any of the 4 panels. These are split into 2 areas:
    • Suggested actions that a GP practice could undertake as a team.
    • Suggested actions for individual patients (by selecting a patient from the panel on the left).
  • Choose whether you want to use NICE or QOF standards from the drop-down menu at the top of the page.
  • If you agree with a suggestion PINGR makes, please click the green thumbs up – this will save this suggestion for you (this can be downloaded as a PDF document at any time for printing or sharing with others).
  • If you do not agree with a suggestion that PINGR makes, please click the red thumbs down – this will remove the suggestion from your screen and provide us with important feedback. The more you can tell us why you don’t agree with the suggestion the better.
  • You can also add your own action plans in addition to the ones suggested by PINGR at the bottom of the suggested action plan lists.
